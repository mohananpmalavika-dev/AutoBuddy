The problem

Local mobility is still too opaque for riders, drivers, and operators.

Riders want trust, not guesswork

Uncertain fares, weak live updates, and inconsistent driver communication make short local trips feel risky and inefficient.

Drivers need fairer economics

High commissions and low transparency push drivers toward offline negotiation, hurting reliability and customer confidence.

Operators lack control

Local teams need KYC, pricing, payouts, refunds, safety events, and service quality in one operational dashboard.

The answer

AutoBuddy is a full-stack ride operating system, not just a booking screen.

We combine consumer booking, driver workflow, and admin-grade governance into one platform built for Kerala launch markets.

Why now

The market is moving toward local, low-commission, driver-friendly models.

India shared mobility tailwinds

Smartphone adoption, UPI payments, high fuel costs, and demand for flexible transport are pushing shared mobility forward.

Projected growth

IMARC projects India's shared mobility market at 6.20% CAGR for 2026-2034.

Auto-rickshaw economics are changing

Major platforms have tested daily or flat fees for auto drivers in India, signaling pressure on high commission models.

AutoBuddy positioning

Fair driver plans, transparent fares, local support, and operational trust as the wedge.

Sources: KSUM Idea Grant page; IMARC India Shared Mobility Market; TechCrunch coverage of Uber India auto-rickshaw fee model.

Business model

Designed for both liquidity and driver trust.

Launch revenue streams

  • Per-ride platform fee or commission on completed rides.
  • Driver onboarding, verification, and subscription plans.
  • Passenger premium options: priority pickup, scheduled rides, corporate billing.
  • Fleet/operator tools for local institutions and enterprise travel.

Unit economics levers

  • Higher driver density lowers wait time and cancellation cost.
  • Local pricing controls allow district, locality, and driver-level rules.
  • Admin dashboards reduce support leakage and manual reconciliation.
  • Subscriptions smooth revenue beyond pure commission pressure.

Go to market

Win one Kerala corridor, then replicate city by city.

Phase 1: Pilot

Kochi / Infopark / metro corridors

Onboard verified drivers, run controlled passenger cohorts, prove response time and completion rate.

Phase 2: Regional density

Thiruvananthapuram, Kozhikode, Thrissur

Use local driver referrals, hotels, hospitals, campuses, and transport hubs as repeat-demand anchors.

Phase 3: Institutional rides

Corporate and fleet channels

Offer admin tools, billing codes, scheduled rides, safety logs, and reporting for partner organizations.
